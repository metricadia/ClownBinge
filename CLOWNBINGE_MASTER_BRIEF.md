## 4. Core Mission

## 5. The Source Standard (Three Tiers)

Every factual claim is verified against a primary source before publication. The platform recognizes three source tiers:

**Tier One — Government and Legal Records**
Official government records, court documents, congressional voting records, federal agency publications, FEC filings, judicial records maintained by the Federal Judicial Center, and official legislative transcripts including the Congressional Record. This is the gold standard. Primary facts trace here.

**Tier Two — Peer-Reviewed and Nonpartisan Research**
Peer-reviewed academic research and nonpartisan research institutions with documented, publicly available methodology. This includes: the Government Accountability Office, the Congressional Budget Office, the Bipartisan Policy Center, and university-based research centers with published sourcing standards.

**Tier Three — Context Sources Only**
Recognized news organizations with published editorial standards, a documented corrections policy, and identifiable editorial leadership. Tier Three sources establish context only. They never establish primary facts.

Primary sources are linked directly within article text. Readers can verify every factual claim in every article independently without leaving the page. ClownBinge does not ask readers to trust it. It asks them to check the source.

---

## 6. The Citation Standard

Every article's citations are stored in the database field `verified_source` in a proprietary format:

`Label :: Description (Year) :: Publisher.`

- Separator between fields: ` :: `
- Separator between entries: `;`
- No URLs in the citation block
- APA 7 title case applied to all citation labels
- Example: `BEA :: National Income and Product Accounts (2023) :: Bureau of Economic Analysis.; BLS :: Current Employment Statistics Survey (2024) :: Bureau of Labor Statistics.`

---

## 7. The Factoid Popup System

Articles contain inline `cb-factoid` class anchor links. These are not navigation links. They are research-grade hover cards that open a popup overlay displaying:

- A title for the source/claim
- A two-section summary (separated by `||`) with verified sourcing detail
- A copy button for citeable text

This allows readers to hover any linked phrase in an article and immediately see the sourced research behind that specific claim, without leaving the page. It is the most distinctive UX feature of the platform — it transforms articles into interactive citation archives rather than flat text documents.

Standard internal cross-article navigation links use plain `<a href="/case/[slug]">` tags with keyword-rich anchor text, not the `cb-factoid` class.

## 8. Legal Architecture

ClownBinge operates under the strongest First Amendment protections available in the United States. *New York Times Co. v. Sullivan*, 376 U.S. 254 (1964) establishes the actual malice standard for public figures. Because ClownBinge publishes only verified primary source documentation, actual malice cannot be established against this platform by design.

The platform does not speculate. It does not fabricate. It cites. The citations are in the article. The article is in the archive.

## 9. What ClownBinge Is Not

- Not a commentary platform
- Not an opinion publication
- Does not characterize, editorialize, or assign motive
- Does not publish anonymous tips or unverified allegations
- Does not cover private individuals — only public figures and institutions that have accepted positions of public trust, public authority, or public financial stewardship

**DB write rule (critical):** Never use `executeSql`. Always use `psql` dollar-quoting or a Node.js script with the direct pg module path. Tags column is `text[]`. Citation field is `verified_source`.

**Source tile rule:** Top 3-4 abbreviated labels from the citation block, joined with `"; "`, hard 44-character cap, no ellipsis.

**Slug format:** Kebab-case, auto-generated from title, used in URLs as `/case/[slug]`

**Stats API:** `/api/posts/stats` filters to CB-prefixed articles only (explicitly excludes CBR founding doc). Cache-Control: no-store. StatWidget staleTime: 0.

## 18. Content Rules and Editorial Style

**No em dashes (—) in prose.** No double hyphens either.

**Sentences must be under 55 words.**

**APA 7 title case** applied to all citation labels.

**Seed file rule:** After every DB insertion, `artifacts/api-server/src/posts-seed.json` must be updated to stay in sync.

**Citation format:** `Label :: Description (Year) :: Publisher.` No URLs in the `verified_source` field. Entries separated by `;`.

**Status field:** Always `'published'` for live articles. Timestamp is `published_at`.

**Tags column:** `text[]` (PostgreSQL array).

**Article body format:** Full HTML. Articles contain `<p>`, `<h2>`, `<h3>`, `<ul>`, `<ol>`, `<blockquote>`, and inline `<a class="cb-factoid">` links for sourced claims, and plain `<a href="/case/[slug]">` for internal cross-article links.

**DB body cleanup:** `regexp_replace(body, '\n\s*\+\s*\n', '\n', 'g')` to fix line-continuation artifacts from psql.
