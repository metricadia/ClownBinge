## Book Purpose

To establish, using only primary source documents, that Judaism (a 3,500-year-old religious and cultural tradition) and Zionism (a 19th-century secular political movement) are distinct, separate, and non-equivalent — and that the conflation of the two is itself a documented political strategy, not a theological truth.

---

## Chapter Outline

### Ch. 1 — Two Traditions, One Conflation
The definitional problem. What Judaism is, what Zionism is, and why the distinction matters forensically. Establishes the book's methodology: primary source only, no editorializing.

### Ch. 2 — The Basel Congress, 1897
Herzl's founding documents, the congress proceedings, the secular-nationalist framing in his own words. Many early Zionist leaders were atheists. The record is unambiguous.

### Ch. 3 — Jewish Anti-Zionism: The Internal Record
Neturei Karta, the American Council for Judaism, rabbinic rulings against Zionism — the opposition from within Jewish tradition, documented continuously since 1897.

### Ch. 4 — The Balfour Declaration
A British foreign policy letter from Foreign Secretary Arthur Balfour to banker Walter Rothschild (1917). Who wrote it, who received it, what the Ottoman and Arab response was. Primary source only.

### Ch. 5 — Land, Census, and Demographic Reality
Ottoman land registries, British Mandate censuses, pre-1948 population distribution. The numbers as recorded, before the politics.

### Ch. 6 — The UN Record
Resolution 3379 (1975) equating Zionism with racism, and its reversal (1991). The political context, the votes, the reasoning on all sides.

### Ch. 7 — Israeli Law: Nation vs. Faith
The legal distinction between Israeli nationality and Jewish nationality in Israeli civil law. Court records and statutory text show the state itself does not treat these as equivalent.

### Ch. 8 — The Conflation Strategy, Documented
When and where equating anti-Zionism with antisemitism became organized policy — lobbying records, legislative campaigns, the IHRA definition and its documented critics (including Jewish scholars).

### Ch. 9 — Voices From Within: Scholars, Rabbis, Organizations
A sourced catalog of Jewish anti-Zionist intellectual and religious output, 1897–present. These voices are not marginal; they are part of the continuous mainstream Jewish record.

### Ch. 10 — The Primary Source Verdict
What the record actually says. No opinion added. No editorial conclusion. The documents stand alone.
