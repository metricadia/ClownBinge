Durkheim's Foundational Observation
Emile Durkheim, in "The Elementary Forms of Religious Life" (1912), made an argument that has not been successfully refuted in the century since. They required bodies to gather, to move together, to direct shared attention toward something larger than any individual.
The sacred, in Durkheim's analysis, is the community's experience of itself as something greater than the sum of its parts. Religion was not invented to separate in-group from out-group. It was invented to expand what the in-group was.
