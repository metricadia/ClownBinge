Program Description
1. Provide an overview of your program. *
EACS has operated basketball clinics at Kent Meridian High School for three years, serving East African immigrant and refugee youth ages 11-18. As families relocate from Seattle to South King County due to rising housing costs, demand has grown. Elite FC, our soccer program launched in 2025, carries an 85-youth waiting list. Both components are free, delivered by bilingual coaches from Somali, Eritrean, and Ethiopian communities. SeaTac anchors the program as EACS headquarters. 52% of participants come from single-parent, mother-led households. Basketball runs September through May at Kent Meridian. Soccer runs April through September on Kent fields. The model is consistent: skill-building paired with mentorship from culturally matched adults who provide stability, language access, and community connection for youth navigating displacement and adolescence simultaneously. 250 youth served to date.
Provide enough detail so we have a solid understanding of your program and impact of your work. Max 1000 characters

2. Who will be served in this program? *
East African immigrant and refugee youth ages 11-18 from low-income families in Kent, Renton, and greater South King County. Participants are primarily from Somali, Eritrean, and Ethiopian communities. Most come from single-parent, mother-led households with limited income and limited access to structured recreation. Many are English Language Learners. Families are increasingly relocating from Seattle due to housing cost pressure.
Describe the population focus for this program (e.g., refugees/immigrants, youth, people experiencing homelessness). Max 500 characters

3. Who provides the services for this program? *
Services are delivered by paid part-time coaches who are bilingual community members from East African backgrounds, supervised by EACS Programs Director Mohamed Ugas. EACS employs a Family Engagement Manager who supports parent communication and retention. Volunteers from the community supplement coaching during tournaments and special events. No contracted third parties are used for direct service delivery.

5. What are the specific, emerging, and/or unique issues for participants in your program? *
Three years ago EACS opened basketball space in Seattle because East African boys needed somewhere safe. No curriculum. No grant. A door opened because the community saw its teenagers drifting toward harm. Today 52% of youth come from single-parent, mother-led households. In Somali, Eritrean, and Ethiopian culture, where the traditional family nucleus carries deep honor, that absence compounds in ways mainstream programs cannot measure. Boys carry shame they cannot name. Mothers carry it too. When no trusted male elder from their own community sees them, holds them accountable, and models integrity, the consequences are predictable. As East African families relocate from Seattle to Kent under housing pressure, these youth lose the informal networks that partially cushioned that reality. Elite FC soccer launched in 2025 from the same logic: 85 kids on a waiting list. The community asked. EACS responded. Both programs exist because the community refused to wait for a funded solution.
Describe the need you are trying to address with this program. Explain the challenges and issues, both ongoing and new, that the population you serve is facing. Max 1000 characters

6. How does your program approach equity in both internal practices and the services you provide to the community? If applicable, what changes or impact have you observed for both your clients and staff as a result of these efforts? *
Equity at EACS is not a policy. It is the organizational DNA. Leadership, staff, and coaches are from the communities served. Somali, Amharic, and Tigrinya are working languages, not accommodations. Program design is driven by the families themselves through sessions led by Family Engagement Manager Hilal Mohamed. When 52% of your participants carry a specific cultural wound, you do not hire outside consultants to interpret it. You hire from inside the community. EACS always has.
